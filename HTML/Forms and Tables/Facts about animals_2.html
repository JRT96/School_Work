Lions

The lion (panthera leo) is a mammal of the family Felidae and one of four "big cats" in the genus panthera. The lion is the second largest feline species, after the tiger. The male lion, easily recognised by his mane, weighs between 150-250 kg(350-550 lb). Females range 120-150 kg(260-330lb). In the wild, lions live for around 10-14 years, while in captivity they can live over 20 years. (from wikipedia)
